# Gypsum Recovery — MVE Analysis

> Phase 2: Walls | Deconstruction Lab | ML Systems
> Question: What form of gypsum generates the most value?

---

## What We're Working With

A 2,500 SF home has ~3,500–5,000 SF of drywall (walls + ceilings). That's roughly **3–8 tons** of gypsum board.

**Our advantage:** Crane-based wall section removal + ground disassembly means we can pull drywall off as intact sheets. Nail holes only — no crushing, no mixing with other C&D. This is not typical demo behavior. It changes the value hierarchy.

---

## Value Hierarchy — Best to Worst Form

| Rank | Form | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Intact sheets (nail holes ok)** | **$3–$8/sheet ($75–$320/ton)** | Direct reuse. Habitat ReStore, contractors, DIY. Our method enables this. |
| 2 | Pelletized/granular (bagged) | $150–$375/ton retail | Requires pelletizer ($50K+). Future play. |
| 3 | Pulverized powder (bulk ag) | $30–$55/ton | Hammer mill + screen. Sell to farmers/nurseries. |
| 4 | Powder to wallboard manufacturers | $10–$20/ton | They prefer new-construction scrap. Reject pre-1978. |
| 5 | Crushed chunks (paper attached) | ~$0 | Only value = avoided tipping fee |

**Intact sheets are the MVE winner.** No processing equipment. No grinding. Just careful removal, stacking, and resale.

## The Nail Hole Question

Nail holes in drywall are cosmetically imperfect but structurally fine. Buyers:

| Buyer | Cares About Nail Holes? | Will Pay? |
|---|---|---|
| Habitat for Humanity ReStore | No — they sell as-is | Yes, $3–$6/sheet |
| Contractors (garage, basement, rental) | No — they're mudding anyway | Yes, $3–$5/sheet |
| DIY / Facebook Marketplace | Some care, most don't at 70% off | Yes, $2–$5/sheet |
| New construction (code jobs) | Yes — won't use | No |

**The pitch:** "Used drywall, nail holes only, no water damage, no mold. 1/2" and 5/8" available. $4/sheet, you pick up."

## Contamination Screen (Must-Do)

| Home Age | Risk | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Post-1981 | Low — modern drywall, modern compound | Visual inspect, sell |
| 1978–1981 | Medium — lead paint possible, asbestos compound possible | Test paint. Test joint compound. |
| Pre-1978 | High — lead paint likely, asbestos compound likely | **Must test before ANY resale or ag use** |

**Lead in paint on drywall** fails the ag heavy metal limit (30 mg/kg max). Pre-1978 sheets with lead paint → can't sell for ag, can't sell for reuse without disclosure. Proper disposal only.

**MVE rule:** Post-1981 homes = green light. Pre-1978 = test first ($30–$50/sample). The test cost is trivial vs the value recovered.

## What to Do With Damaged/Partial Sheets

Sheets that break during removal → don't trash them. Two paths:

### Path A: Contract pickup ($0 processing, save on disposal)

| Recycler | Location | Tipping Fee | Service Area |
|---|---|---|---|
| **USA Gypsum** | Pennsylvania | ~$23/ton | 9 states incl. New England |
| **Dynamic Waste Systems** | North Andover, MA | Call for quote | Eastern MA, southern NH, **RI** |

You save $40–$77/ton vs landfill just by source-separating and calling a recycler.

### Path B: On-site crush → ag powder (future, needs volume)

- Hammer mill + trommel screen (~$15K–$50K used)
- Output: 93% gypsum powder / 7% paper shred
- Sell powder to local farms/nurseries at $30–$55/ton
- **Same winter-processing logic as shingles** — batch, stockpile, process when crew has bandwidth
- Only makes sense at 50+ tons/year (10+ homes)

## Crane Workflow Note

**The crane never stops.** Wall panels are lifted off and laid on their side in a staging area elsewhere on the property. Ground crew disassembles later — drywall removal, lumber separation, sheathing, etc. all happen AFTER the crane has moved on to the floor system. The crane is the most expensive asset on site. Every minute it waits is money burned.

## MVE Strategy — Keep It Simple

**Now (1–2 homes/year):**
1. Remove drywall as intact sheets during ground disassembly
2. Stack on pallets by size, cover with tarp
3. Sell intact sheets: ReStore, FB Marketplace, contractor network
4. Damaged sheets → source-separate, contract pickup by Dynamic Waste (~$23/ton vs $100 landfill)
5. Net: **$560–$1,435 swing per home** vs landfilling

**At 3–5 homes/year:**
- Invest in grinder + bagger ($15–$20K)
- Process damaged sheets into powder, bag in 40-lb bags
- Sell to garden centers at $6–$10/bag ($450–$1,500/ton)
- Equipment pays for itself in 2–10 homes
- Same winter-processing logic as shingles — shared site, shared crew

**At 10+ homes/year:**
- Add bulk ag sales (truckload to farms at $30–$50/ton)
- Compost facility contracts (Earth Care Farm, Black Earth)
- Bagged retail continues as highest-margin channel
