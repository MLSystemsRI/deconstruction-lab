Developer Profit — The Q10 Revelation

The single most important answer: "There is no minimum or maximum developer profit."

The purchase price cap is $399,999. TDC per unit is separate. The gap between total funding (grant + sales proceeds) and total expenses is developer profit.

ML Systems Unit Pro Forma
Grant award:$70,000
Sale price (to buyer):$350,000
Total funding:$420,000

Land / site acquisition:−$60,000
Decon of existing structure:−$15,000
New construction (hard cost):−$250,000
Soft costs:−$30,000
Total expenses:−$355,000

DEVELOPER PROFIT:$65,000/unit

Without decon reuse: Hard costs are $262K instead of $250K. Profit drops to $53K. Or you need $82K in grant instead of $70K.

The decon savings flow to TWO places: lower subsidy ask (scoring advantage) AND higher developer margin. You decide the split.

The 51% Resale Value

How the grant accelerates the 51% resale value target.

Without the Grant

Selling recovered materials through multiple channels (open house, ReStore, FB Marketplace, scrap yards). Takes time — building buyer networks, hauling, listing, negotiating.

With the Grant

You are your own buyer. Recovered materials go directly into the new home. No sales channel needed. The "resale" is internal — value captured as avoided cost.

These materials go directly into the new affordable homes we build on the same sites — reducing per-unit development costs by $15,000–$30,000 compared to conventional demolition and new-material construction. We need less subsidy per unit because the old house funds the new one."
