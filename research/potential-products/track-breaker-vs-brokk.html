When You'd Use Only One

Track Breaker Only

Small job, 1–2 walls, no full foundation removal. Cut an egress window opening, remove a single wall section. Use handheld breaker for base cleanup.

Brokk Only

Full demolition where panel recovery doesn't matter. Commercial teardown, no material reuse goal. Walls become rubble — more haul-off, zero reuse value.

Bottom Line

The Product
Track Breaker
  • Build or buy: Build (no commercial equivalent)
  • When: Now — V0 proves physics for ~$10K
  • Role: Precision scoring (the scalpel)
  • IP value: Patent-worthy, licensable, data-generating
  • Lucent Lens: Crew-portable, 2 humans carry it

Brokk 170
  • Build or buy: Buy used (~$100K) or rent
  • When: After 3–4 projects justify ownership
  • Role: General demolition (the hammer)
  • IP value: Zero — commodity
  • Lucent Lens: Remote-operated, replaces 22 laborers
  • Revenue: None — cost center only

The Track Breaker is the product. The Brokk is the tool. Without the Track Breaker, the Brokk just bashes walls into rubble — recoverable as RCA but not as panels. With the Track Breaker, every wall becomes inventory.
