How V0a Works

  1. Set one pivot anchor (8-10" rod into concrete, top of wall)
  2. Bolt steel rail to pivot — rail hangs against wall face
  3. Grab T-handle, swing rail to fracture line position
  4. Start breaker — carriage feeds down rail under operator control
  5. Line done → grab T-handle, swing rail to next position
  6. Repeat until swing arc is exhausted (3–4 lines)
  7. Move pivot to next wall section, continue
Key insight: One steel rail, one pivot, one T-handle. The operator swings the rail to reposition and leans weight into it during breaking. No automation. Pure mechanical simplicity.

How It Works

Five steps. Two workers. No crane, no excavator, no heavy equipment.

Step 1: Carry

Two workers carry the frame to the wall. The frame splits into two sections at a bolted flange — 41 lbs per person. No crane, no forklift.

~2 min

Step 2: Anchor

Drill 4 holes with a hammer drill (~2 min). Set adhesive anchors on top (Hilti HIT-HY 200, seismic-rated). Wedge anchors on bottom. Bolt the frame to the wall face.

Step 3: Mount

Slide the carriage onto the rail. Pin-lock it in place (30 sec). Connect hydraulic hoses from the power pack via quick-connect fittings (15 sec). Start the power pack.

Step 4: Break

Breaker fires at 1,400 BPM. Carriage feeds down the track. 75J impacts fracture a clean vertical line through 8" of concrete. Broken pieces fall away — gravity clears the debris. No scooping, no cleanup.

4-16 min per line

Step 5: Swing & Repeat

V0a: Grab the T-handle. Swing the rail 4 feet to the next line (~1 min). Break again. 3–4 lines per pivot position. Move pivot only when arc is exhausted. After all vertical lines are cut, wall sections tip outward or get crane-lifted as panels.

~1 min swing (vs ~10 min V0 reposition)

Patent Scope

A crew-portable, wall-anchored, track-guided hydraulic breaking system combining:

  1. 1 Crew-portable, wall-anchored track system
  2. 2 Hydraulic breaker mounted on linear carriage
  3. 3 One-sided concrete anchoring (no back-side access required)
  4. 4 Gravity-assisted debris clearing (vertical operation)
  5. 5 Self-locking drive mechanism (Acme lead screw)
  6. 6 Camera-based fracture monitoring and feed control
  7. 7 Modular breaker head (swappable energy levels)
No prior art found. No commercial product exists in this space.
