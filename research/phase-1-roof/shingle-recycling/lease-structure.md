## Core Concept

Don't buy the grinder outright. **Lease it.** Monthly payments turn a $75K–$80K capital event into an operational line item — and unlock a multi-party value flow where every stakeholder profits from a single ton of shingles.

**Winter-only grinding.** We aggregate shingles year-round but only process in winter (Nov–Mar). Below 40°F asphalt cement becomes brittle — it fractures clean instead of gumming up hammers. RI winters (avg 15–35°F Dec–Feb) are a free cryogenic advantage. Less wear on equipment, cleaner product, lower fuel consumption per ton. The cold is the value generator.

**The neural connection:** This shingle arm feeds data, relationships, and revenue into the larger deconstruction platform. Every roofer who shows up becomes an awareness vector for the secondary lumber market. Every ton processed generates ontology data. Every dollar flows through the Lucent Lens — humans profit first.

## Operating Cycle — Aggregate Year-Round, Grind in Winter

```
APR–OCT (roofing season)         NOV–MAR (grinding season)
─────────────────────────        ─────────────────────────
Roofers bring tear-offs    →→→   Grinder runs. Cold = clean fracture.
Stockpile grows on site          Process entire stockpile.
No grinding. No fuel.            Sell RAS to HMA plants (spring paving).
Lease + insurance only.          Revenue concentrated here.
```

**Why winter only:**
- Below 40°F, asphalt cement is brittle — grinds to spec with less energy and less wear
- Hammers last longer (asphalt doesn't gum/coat), screens clog less
- Roofing season is slow — crew has bandwidth to operate the grinder
- HMA plants buy RAS in late winter/early spring for paving season
- Stockpile doesn't agglomerate (clump) in cold — no sand additive needed

## The 51% + 25% Unlock

### 51% — Resale Value Captured

The core Decon Lab input: 51% of recovered building materials can be resold.

For shingles specifically:
- Input: Tear-off shingles (waste in current system, $0 value)
- Output: RAS product at $35/ton
- Virgin AC replacement value: $350–$500/ton
- **We're capturing ~7–10% of the virgin value** — room to grow as market matures

This is the first proof point for the broader thesis: building materials have resale value that's currently being landfilled.

### 25% — Land Lease Revenue

Build site staging area doubles as aggregation yard (Apr–Oct) and grinding pad (Nov–Mar). No new land cost — dual-use of land already in the build budget. Future: dedicated quarter-acre lease ($800–$1,500/mo) as volume scales beyond build-site capacity.
