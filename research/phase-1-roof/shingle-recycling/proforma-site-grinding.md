### 2–4 Day Site Operation — What Can We Generate?

**Roofer inflow assumptions (peak season, July–September):**

| Scenario | Roofers/Day | Tons/Day | 2-Day Total | 4-Day Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative (3 roofers) | 3 loads × 4 tons | 12 tons | 24 tons | 48 tons |
| Moderate (6 roofers) | 6 loads × 4 tons | 24 tons | 48 tons | 96 tons |
| Strong (10 roofers) | 10 loads × 4 tons | 40 tons | 80 tons | 160 tons |

The Morbark 950 processes 8–15 tons/hr. Even the strong scenario (40 tons/day) is only 3–5 hours of grinding. **The bottleneck is inflow, not processing.**

## RIRRC Contact Strategy

**Sal has a contact at RIRRC.** This is the Lucent Lens at work — local relationship before market infrastructure.

### What to ask RIRRC contact:

1. **Exact current C&D tipping fee** (estimated $95–$130/ton, need the real number)
2. **Annual shingle tonnage received** (they track by material category)
3. **Would RIRRC support a diversion program?** (they WANT less C&D — landfill runs out 2046)
4. **Would they refer roofers to you?** (sign at the scalehouse, flyer in the packet)
5. **Any existing Beneficial Use Determination precedent** for shingles in RI?
6. **Would they co-brand or endorse?** (RIRRC logo on your pitch = instant credibility)

### Why RIRRC should say yes:
- Every ton you divert extends their landfill life
- C&D volume already dropped 75% (517K → 132K tons) — they're actively pushing this
- You're solving their problem WITH a local business, not an out-of-state competitor
- First shingle recycler in RI = PR win for RIRRC's sustainability metrics
