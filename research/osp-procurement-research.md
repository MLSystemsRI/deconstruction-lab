## What Is OSP

**Ocean State Procures (OSP)** is the State of Rhode Island's eProcurement
system, operated by the RI Division of Purchases (ridop.ri.gov). All state
agency purchasing flows through this platform.

**Key URLs:**
- Website: https://ridop.ri.gov
- Bid Board: https://ridop.ri.gov/vendors/bidding-opportunities
- MPA Contract Board: https://ridop.ri.gov/master-price-agreements/mpa-contract-board-search
- CMAR Sub Opportunities: https://ridop.ri.gov/vendors/cmar-sub-contractor-opportunities
- Vendor Training Center: https://ridop.ri.gov (training resources section)
- RIVIP External Solicitations: https://ridop.ri.gov/vendor-resources/all-solicitations

**Registration:** Free. Requires FEIN, W-9, UNSPSC commodity codes, diversity
info. Self-managed. Can register with "pending" status to view and respond
to bids. Full "approved" status requires W-9 upload + payment method.

## Master Price Agreements (MPAs)

- Pre-competed contracts creating **qualified vendor pools**
- Once on MPA, agencies send mini-bids directly to qualified vendors
- **Administrative fee:** 0.33% of contract value (reduced from 1% as of Jan 2026)
- **200+ MPAs** across diverse categories
- Quasi-agencies, municipalities, school districts can piggyback
- Search active MPAs at the MPA Contract Board

**Target MPA categories:**
- Environmental services / demolition
- Construction services / general contracting
- Building materials / lumber / structural
- Recycling and waste management

## CMAR Subcontracting

Construction-Management-at-Risk (CMAR) is for projects $5M+ where the state
selects a prime contractor during design. The prime bids out all trade
subcontracts.

**Current CMAR primes in RI:** Dimeo Construction, Gilbane, Shawmut, others.

**Active:** Dimeo — URI Bay Campus Phase 2 (subcontracting via BuildingConnected).

**How to participate:**
1. Register on BuildingConnected
2. Monitor CMAR Sub-Contractor Opportunities page on ridop.ri.gov
3. Network with primes — they need MBE subs to meet 15% goal
4. Target scopes: demolition, concrete, structural steel, material salvage

## MBE Certification

Administered by **Division of Equity, Diversity and Inclusion (DEDI)**.

**Benefits:**
- 15% of all state contract dollars must go to MBE/WBE firms
- Providence: 20% combined MBE+WBE goals
- 3% VBE participation goal statewide
- Primes actively seek MBE subs to meet participation requirements

**Requirements:**
- 51% owned by qualifying minority individuals
- Principal owner 3-year average AGI < $350,000
- Firm average gross receipts < $23.98M
- Personal net worth < $1,320,000

**Process:** Free, ~90 days, online at ridedi.prismcompliance.com.
Contact: DEDI.MBECO@doa.ri.gov

## Key Finding: Zero Deconstruction Contracts

Rhode Island currently treats all building removal as "demolition." There are
**no deconstruction-specific contracts** in the state procurement system.

**Opportunity:** ML Systems can be the first to frame material recovery as an
alternative to demolition — lower tipping fees, sustainability compliance,
80-90% recovery vs. 100% landfill. This is market creation, not competition.
